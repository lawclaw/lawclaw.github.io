Oskar Schindler

The man that saved Jewish people from the Nazis

Brief summary:

  • 1908 - Born on April 28th in Austria-Hungary
  • 1920 - Works as a salesman for his father's factory for eight years.
  • 1938 - Becomes a German intel.
  • 1939 - World War 2 begins.
  • 1940 - Starts his factory in the Krakow neighbourhood of Zablocie.
  • 1940 - Accepts Jewish workers at his factory.
  • 1942 - Schindler goes to the station to rescue his business manager, Abraham Bankier, and his other workers from being deported to Belzec.
  • 1944 - Schindler's list. Those who are on the list are sent away.
  • 1945, May 8th - The Jews give Schindler a gold ring with the inscription: "He who saves a single life saves the entire world".
  • 1945 - Schindler and his wife along with some Jews flee to the American front.
  • 1974 - Schindler dies October 9th

Beyond this day, no thinking person could fail to see what would happen.

- Oskar Schindler
